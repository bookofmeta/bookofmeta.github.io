Family leadership

Accepting a family member as a leader is unpopular in the modern world. People readily accept a politician as their leader. But they don’t accept a spouse or a parent as their leader.

This is due to the popularity of personal responsibility in the modern world. Unfortunately, not everyone is born with the strength to take responsibility for themselves. For those who don’t have this strength, personal responsibility becomes a burden rather than a freedom.

Leadership in the family traditionally built strength in such people. It allowed people to offload their personal responsibility to a family leader until they became strong on their own.

As family leadership vanishes in the modern world, more and more people will suffer under the burden of personal responsibility.
