Seeking approval

What is approval seeking?

Approval seeking is a behaviour which stems from low self esteem. Self esteem is your evaluation of your worth. You define your worth as your value as a person compared to others. When you value yourself low you have low self esteem. Your low valuation of yourself makes you look for high evaluation from others. You disregard you own evaluation when you get high evaluation from others. You behave in particular ways to invite high evaluation from others. These behaviour in essence seek approval from others that your value as a person is high. Thus they are called approval seeking behaviour. In this behaviour you try to be liked, admired by others or feel superior to others. You hate to be disliked, looked down or feel inferior.

Examples of approval seeking behaviour

Trying to get liked

People pleasing
You like making others happy in spite of not having genuine empathy for them. You laugh at their unfunny jokes, you agree with whatever they say, you are eager to help them, you buy them gifts, you like delivering good news to them, you hate delivering bad news, you feel happy with they are happy, you feel uncomfortable when they are unhappy. You want to make them happy so that they will like you. Whey they like you, your self esteem goes up.

Like to conform
You like doing things which are approved by society. Your opinions confirm with the conventional wisdom. Your choices reflect societal standards. You choose society approved career and society approved spouse. You keep up with latest trends. Your ambitions are driven by society. You publicly display emotions which are approved by society. You like to get angry because society labels its at strength. You like to conform because you fear being disliked by society if you go against it’s norms.

Trying to get admired

Displaying success
You display your successes to get compliments. You post on social media about your successes to fish for compliments. You show off your expensive phone, exotic vacations and attractive spouse. You even show off successes of a friend or a family member to get compliments. Your connection to them makes you take partial credit for their success. Your self esteem goes up when you get compliments, even when it is for a friend or family member.

Displaying beauty and intelligence
You use your physical beauty to get admired by others. You post beautiful pictures of yourself on social media to get compliments. You like to wear outfits which get compliments from others. Your self esteem goes up when you get compliments.

Displaying intelligence
You use your intelligence to get admired by others. You like to post intellectual articles on social media. You like talking to your friends about reading intellectual movies and books. Your self esteem goes up instantly when you display intelligence as you are sure that society admires intelligence.

Displaying virtue
You show virtue to get admiration. You post on social media about your outrage on someone else’s injustice. You publicly display empathy for victims of a natural calamity. You display virtue only to get admiration, you do not have genuine empathy for others. Your self esteem goes up instantly when you display virtue as you are sure that society admires it virtue.

Trying to feel superior

Finding fault in others
You like to find mistakes made by others. You readily point them out at every opportunity. You don’t care about hurting others feelings by doing it. You do it even to a friend or family member. You feel superior when pointing our mistakes by others. You self esteem goes up as you proved to yourself that your superior than the person who made a mistake.

Insulting and making fun of others
You like to insult others. You insult people for not being smart, beautiful or successful. You like to insult them when they make mistakes. You like to make fun of their mistakes, lack of intelligence, beauty or success . You like it when people feel hurt by your insults and jokes. You take it as proof of your superiority. You self esteem goes up as you proved to yourself that your superior than the person who you insulted or made fun of.

Proving others wrong
You like to prove opinions and beliefs of others as wrong. You like to point out logical inconsistencies in their opinions and beliefs. When you cannot find logical inconsistencies you insult them as not smart. You self esteem goes up when you prove others’ opinions and beliefs wrong as you feel superior for holding correct opinions and beliefs.

You like to be with people who are admired by others
You associate yourself with people who are admired by others. You self esteem instantly goes up when you associate with them as you allow yourself to take partial credit for their admiration.

Feeling happy at failure of others
You are happy to see other people fail, even when it is a family member or friend. It makes you feel superior as you think that the person failing is inferior to you. You are jealous of others’ success. You hates to give credit or compliment for success of others. You feel inferior when others experience success because you feel inferior for not being successful yourself. Your self esteem goes up when others experience failure, it goes down with others experience success.

Bad listening
You don’t listen to others in conversation. Instead of using conversation to understand someone others you use it to feel superior than them. So you will change a conversation into an argument to prove the other person wrong. You love to argue as it gives you a chance to win it and feel superior. Consequently you hate to lose arguments. Your self esteem goes up when you win arguments, it goes down when you lost arguments.

Buy expensive brands
You buy clothes and gadgets of luxury brands. You don’t care if the clothes are comfortable or if the gadgets are low on utility. You feel superior for owning these products as society them as an indicator of a person’s high value. Your self esteem goes up instantly when you buy these products.

Sensitive to criticism
You are easily offended by criticism. It makes you feel inferior as think that when others criticise you they are devaluing you as a person. So reject the criticism and get defensive. You self esteem goes down instantly when others criticise you.

Sensitive to disrespect
You are easily offended when others disrespect you. It doesn’t matter if it is from by a stranger, friend or a family member. You especially don’t like it if a person you admire disrespects you. Disrespect offends you because it makes you feel inferior. You think others are devaluing you a person. You instinctively fight back to disrespect in an attempt to increase your value in the eyes of others. You self esteem goes down instantly when others disrespect you.

Hate to lose
You hate to lose any competition. You see competitions as a public indicator of a person’s value. So when you lose your feel inferior. You will refuse to accept defeat in order to gain some value in the eyes of others. You will to blame external factors for your loss. You will not accept responsibility for your defeat. Your self respect goes up when you win, it goes down when you lose.

Why is approval seeking not a good strategy for improving self esteem?

Approval seeking is not a good as it puts your self esteem under the control of others. It is also not a good strategy as people have an incentive not to give you approval. This is because they themselves seek approval and feel giving approval to others diminishes reduces their self esteem. The biggest problem with approval seeking is that it’s a never ending pursuit. Any increase in self esteem from approval will be short lived. You will need to constantly seek approval to maintain your self esteem. For e.g. if you post a beautiful picture of yourself on social media and get plenty of compliments for it, the increase in self esteem from it will be momentary. In a short time you will lose the boost in self esteem from those compliments and will need to upload another beautiful picture to get compliments and regain the lost self esteem. You will end up needing to post beautiful pictures of yourself regularly. The constant need to get approval from others has a tremendous the quality of your life. It makes you invest energy throughout your life to get approval. You might spend a big portion of your life putting spending energy purely to get approval. This investment doesn’t give any sustainable return as your self esteem is always rest after approval. This results in a life time of unhappiness caused by a futile pursuit of self esteem. In contrast, if you don’t seek approval you can use your energy in pursuits which you do for your own happiness and not for approval from society.

How to stop seeking approval

The first step in stopping to seek approval is realising its futility. Realise that your value as a person is intrinsic. Every person is valuable by default, including you. Lose all notions of some people being better than others for any reason. Once you realize this then you can use your self awareness and self control and control your approval seeking behavior. Your approval seeking behaviour will not stop automatically once your realize its futility. It is part of a human insecurity which will always remain. But you can control that behaviour using self control. That will make your self esteem independent of what others think of you. To control that behaviour use your self awareness to catch yourself when you do something for approval. Then use your will power to stop yourself from doing it. It will difficult to do this in the beginning, but once you see some success you will gain confidence. After that it will be easier to stop yourself from seeking approval. But it is a life long task. You need to be always on the look out of needing to seeking approval from others. Take a stand to never allow yourself to do it.
